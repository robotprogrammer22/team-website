In 1999, Team 60 played at two events. At the NASA Ames Regional at Moffet Field in California, Team 60 received the “Most Photogenic” award. They later traveled to Orlando Florida and competed at the National Championship where they placed 86th.

The competition “Double Trouble” was played by two alliances at a time, with each alliance composed of two teams. Points were scored by positioning “floppies,” robots, and the “puck” on the playing field. Floppies are lightweight, pillow-like objects with Velcro-loop material located in the center and around the perimeter. Each alliance has ten color coded floppies located on the playing field and at the player stations. At the end of each two minute match, points were awarded as follows: Each two-team alliance receives one point for each of its floppies that is at least 2” over and not touching the playing field surface, and less than eight feet above the surface if the playing field. Each alliance receives three points for each of its floppies eight feet or higher over the surface of the playing field. Any robot that climbs onto the puck will multiply its alliance’s score by three.
