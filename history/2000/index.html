The year 2000 was a remarkable year for Team 60. At the NASA Ames Regional in California, the team outperformed all the other teams during qualification matches to become the #1 seed for playoffs. Team 60 then went on to win the Regional where about 40 teams competed. Alliance members included The Cheesy Poofs(254) and Team 409. Team 60 also received the Quality Award at the regional.

At the National Championship in Orlando Florida, Team 60 continued to perform well and earned the #5 seed for playoffs out of 250 teams.

In the competition game “Co-opertition FIRST”, four teams, paired in two alliances, will compete in each match. An alliance scores points by placing balls in their goal, and by positioning their robots in designated areas at the end of each match. At the start of a match each alliance has seven yellow balls and one black ball in their station. In addition, there are fifteen yellow balls and two black balls on the far side of the field which may be scored by either alliance.
